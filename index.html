What is this?
This is an interactive knowledge graph that maps every major Claude concept, tool, and workflow relevant to technical writers — and shows how they connect to each other. It was built as a learning resource to answer one question: where exactly does Claude fit into a technical writing workflow?
Most AI guides for writers are blog posts or slide decks. This is different — it's a living, explorable map that you navigate at your own pace, with copyable prompts at every node so learning immediately translates into action.

How to use it
1
Tap ▶ Guide and pick your role — New to Claude, Technical Writer, Docs Lead, or Developer. You'll get a walkthrough built for you.
2
The guide moves the camera node-by-node. Each stop has a narration explaining why this concept matters, and a prompt you can copy and use in Claude immediately.
3
Tap any node to explore freely. The panel shows what it is, how it connects to other nodes, and a direct link to try it in Claude.
4
Use the search bar to jump to any concept instantly. Use the cluster legend on the left to isolate a category and fly the camera to it.
5
The three learning paths (Beginner, Writer-Specific, Advanced) highlight a sequence of nodes as a suggested learning order.
Why it was built
Technical writers are among the professionals with the most to gain from Claude — and often among the last to adopt it systematically. Most AI education focuses on developers. This graph was built to change that: to give writers a complete, honest map of what Claude can do in their specific workflow, from first draft to DITA XML to L10n to CI/CD pipelines.
Every node represents a real capability gap that writers face. Every prompt was written to be immediately useful — not generic, but specific to the documentation context a technical writer would actually be working in.
